## PUBLIC SPACES MANAGEMENT AND COMMUNITY USE POLICY

**Policy Number:** PSM-2024-07  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Review Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Approved By:** Riverdale City Council  
**Resolution Number:** 2024-RC-1892  

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose
This policy establishes a comprehensive framework for the management, maintenance, and community use of public spaces within the City of Riverdale. The policy aims to ensure equitable access, sustainable management, and the preservation of public spaces for current and future generations while promoting community wellbeing and environmental stewardship.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to all public spaces owned, operated, or managed by the City of Riverdale, including but not limited to:
- Municipal parks and gardens
- Public squares and plazas
- Community recreation facilities
- Nature reserves and conservation areas
- Public walkways and trails
- Waterfront areas and beaches
- Sports fields and courts
- Children's playgrounds

### 1.3 Legislative Authority
This policy is established pursuant to the Local Government Act 1995, the Environmental Protection Act 2019, and the Community Services Regulation 2021, and operates in conjunction with all relevant state and federal legislation.

---

## 2. DEFINITIONS

For the purposes of this policy, the following definitions apply:

**"Authorized Officer"** means any person appointed by the City Manager to enforce provisions of this policy, including but not limited to park rangers, compliance officers, and designated council staff.

**"Commercial Activity"** means any activity conducted for profit or commercial gain, including but not limited to vending, fitness classes, photography services, and promotional events.

**"Community Event"** means a gathering or activity organized for public benefit, cultural celebration, or community engagement, whether free or ticketed.

**"Public Space"** means any area of land or water, and associated facilities, owned or managed by the City of Riverdale that is accessible to the general public.

**"Permit"** means written authorization issued by the City of Riverdale for specific activities in designated public spaces.

## 3. POLICY PRINCIPLES

### 3.1 Guiding Principles
The management of public spaces shall be guided by the following principles:

a) **Accessibility and Inclusion**: Public spaces shall be accessible to all members of the community regardless of age, ability, cultural background, or socioeconomic status.

b) **Environmental Sustainability**: Management practices shall prioritize ecological preservation, biodiversity protection, and climate resilience.

c) **Community Benefit**: Public spaces shall be managed to maximize community wellbeing, social cohesion, and quality of life.

d) **Safety and Security**: All public spaces shall be maintained to ensure public safety and minimize risks to users.

e) **Cultural Heritage**: Recognition and protection of sites of cultural, historical, and indigenous significance.

### 3.2 Management Objectives
The City of Riverdale commits to:
- Maintaining public spaces to a high standard of cleanliness, safety, and functionality
- Providing diverse recreational opportunities for all age groups and abilities
- Protecting and enhancing natural ecosystems and urban biodiversity
- Facilitating appropriate community and commercial use through transparent permit systems
- Ensuring sustainable funding and resource allocation for ongoing maintenance

---

## 4. PERMITTED AND PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

### 4.1 General Permitted Activities
The following activities are permitted in public spaces without requiring specific authorization:
- Passive recreation (walking, sitting, reading)
- Children's play in designated areas
- Non-organized sports and exercise
- Photography for personal use
- Picnics and small gatherings (under 20 people)
- Dog walking in designated areas with appropriate control and waste disposal

### 4.2 Activities Requiring Permits
The following activities require prior written permission through the appropriate permit process:
- Organized events with more than 20 participants
- Commercial activities of any nature
- Installation of temporary structures or equipment
- Amplified sound or music
- Drone operation
- Professional photography or filming
- Weddings and formal ceremonies
- Sporting competitions and tournaments
- Educational programs and workshops

### 4.3 Prohibited Activities
The following activities are strictly prohibited in all public spaces unless specifically authorized:
- Camping or overnight stays
- Lighting fires outside designated facilities
- Consumption of alcohol except in licensed areas or with specific event permits
- Operation of motorized vehicles outside designated areas
- Damage to vegetation, wildlife, or infrastructure
- Littering or illegal dumping
- Weapons or fireworks
- Gambling activities
- Smoking in smoke-free zones

## 5. PERMIT SYSTEM AND FEES

### 5.1 Permit Application Process
Applications for permits must be submitted through the City's online portal or at the Customer Service Center at least:
- 14 days prior for standard activities
- 30 days prior for major events (over 100 participants)
- 60 days prior for events requiring road closures or significant infrastructure

### 5.2 Assessment Criteria
Permit applications will be assessed based on:
- Compatibility with the designated use of the space
- Impact on other users and neighboring properties
- Environmental considerations
- Public safety and risk management
- Availability of facilities and resources
- Compliance with insurance and indemnity requirements

### 5.3 Fee Structure
Fees for public space use shall be determined according to the Council's adopted Fees and Charges Schedule, with consideration for:
- Commercial versus non-profit status
- Size and duration of activity
- Infrastructure and services required
- Environmental impact and remediation needs

### 5.4 Fee Waivers and Reductions
The City Manager may approve fee waivers or reductions for:
- Registered non-profit organizations conducting community benefit activities
- Educational institutions conducting learning programs
- Cultural events promoting diversity and inclusion
- Activities supporting Council's strategic objectives

---

## 6. ENFORCEMENT AND COMPLIANCE

### 6.1 Monitoring and Inspection
Authorized Officers shall conduct regular inspections of public spaces to ensure compliance with this policy and associated regulations. Officers have the authority to:
- Issue warnings for minor breaches
- Issue infringement notices for specified offenses
- Order cessation of unauthorized activities
- Confiscate items used in breach of this policy
- Refer serious matters to law enforcement agencies

### 6.2 Penalties and Infringements
Breaches of this policy may result in:
- Verbal or written warnings
- Infringement notices with prescribed penalties
- Cancellation or suspension of permits
- Recovery of costs for damage or remediation
- Legal proceedings for serious or repeated offenses

### 6.3 Appeals Process
Persons aggrieved by decisions made under this policy may lodge an appeal in writing to the City Manager within 14 days of notification. Appeals will be reviewed by the appropriate committee or delegated authority, with determinations provided within 28 days.

## 7. MAINTENANCE AND ASSET MANAGEMENT

### 7.1 Maintenance Standards
The City shall maintain public spaces according to adopted service level agreements, including:
- Regular mowing, pruning, and horticultural maintenance
- Daily cleaning and waste removal in high-use areas
- Prompt repair of damaged infrastructure
- Regular safety inspections of play equipment and facilities
- Graffiti removal within specified timeframes

### 7.2 Asset Renewal and Upgrade
The City shall implement systematic asset management practices including:
- Regular condition assessments
- Planned renewal programs
- Community consultation on significant upgrades
- Integration of universal design principles
- Climate adaptation measures

### 7.3 Environmental Management
Environmental management practices shall include:
- Integrated pest management with minimal chemical use
- Water-sensitive urban design principles
- Native plant preference in landscaping
- Habitat creation and protection
- Waste reduction and recycling programs

## 8. COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT AND PARTNERSHIPS

### 8.1 Consultation Requirements
The City shall undertake community consultation for:
- Major changes to public space use or configuration
- Development of masterplans and management plans
- Introduction of new fees or charges
- Significant policy amendments

### 8.2 Volunteer Programs
The City encourages community participation through:
- Friends of Parks groups
- Bushcare and environmental restoration programs
- Community garden initiatives
- Event volunteering opportunities
- Adopt-a-Park schemes

### 8.3 Partnerships
The City may enter into partnerships with community organizations, businesses, and other government agencies to:
- Enhance public space amenities
- Deliver programs and services
- Share resources and expertise
- Secure additional funding
- Promote activation and use

## 9. IMPLEMENTATION AND REVIEW

### 9.1 Implementation Plan
This policy shall be implemented through:
- Staff training and awareness programs
- Public information campaigns
- Updated operational procedures
- Integration with Council's digital platforms
- Regular reporting to Council

### 9.2 Performance Monitoring
Key performance indicators shall include:
- Public space utilization rates
- Permit application processing times
- Maintenance service level achievement
- Community satisfaction surveys
- Environmental health indicators
- Safety incident statistics

### 9.3 Policy Review
This policy shall be reviewed biennially or as required due to:
- Legislative changes
- Significant community feedback
- Operational requirements
- Council strategic plan updates
